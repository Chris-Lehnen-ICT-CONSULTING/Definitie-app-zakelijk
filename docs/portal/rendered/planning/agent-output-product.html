Product Manager Crisis Analysis: DefinitieAgent Brownfield Recovery

Executive Summary: The Hard Truths

The DefinitieAgent project is experiencing catastrophic scope failure with only 14.8% completion after consuming significant resources. With 290 user stories across 32 EPICs and an estimated 3.7 years to completion at current velocity, this €800k investment is at critical risk.

Hard Truth #1: The project has suffered from unchecked feature creep. The 9:1 story-to-epic ratio reveals over-granulation that has paralyzed delivery.

Hard Truth #2: At current burn rate (3 stories/sprint), the ROI will be negative for at least 2 years, with opportunity costs exceeding €1.2M.

Hard Truth #3: Without radical intervention, this project will become a textbook example of public sector IT failure.

PM Verdict: CONDITIONAL APPROVAL - The recovery plan is viable BUT requires immediate stakeholder buy-in for a 90% scope reduction and brutal prioritization.

Business Impact Analysis

Financial Impact

  • **Investment at Risk**: €800,000 direct costs
  • **Opportunity Cost**: €400,000/year (team could deliver 2-3 smaller products)
  • **Delay Cost**: €50,000/month in manual processes that continue without automation
  • **Total Risk Exposure**: €1.6M over 24 months

Stakeholder Impact

  • **Government Credibility**: Another failed IT project damages digital transformation initiatives
  • **Team Morale**: 85% likely to experience burnout without intervention
  • **User Trust**: Legal professionals waiting 18+ months for promised functionality
  • **Political Risk**: Potential audit findings on mismanaged public funds

Market Impact

  • **Competitive Window**: 12-18 months before alternatives emerge
  • **First-Mover Advantage**: Already lost to manual processes entrenchment
  • **Adoption Risk**: Users may reject overly complex solution

Root Cause Analysis: How We Got Here

1. Requirements Explosion (The Feature Factory Trap)

The project fell into the classic "everything is important" trap. Without a clear product owner enforcing trade-offs, every stakeholder request became a user story. The evidence:

  • 32 EPICs where 8 would suffice
  • 290 stories for a single-user application
  • No clear MVP definition until crisis point

2. Technical Over-Engineering

  • 51 services for basic CRUD + AI operations (10-15 would suffice)
  • God Objects with 1,400+ lines indicating poor separation of concerns
  • 7,250 prompt tokens with 83% duplication

3. Absence of Product Discipline

  • No Definition of Done enforcement
  • No velocity tracking until crisis
  • No regular stakeholder reality checks
  • Missing "NO" muscle - everything was "YES, we'll add that"

4. Perfectionism Over Pragmatism

  • 45 validation rules before basic functionality complete
  • Security/compliance features for a non-production system
  • Export to 5 formats when users need just one

PM Recommendation: CONDITIONAL APPROVAL
