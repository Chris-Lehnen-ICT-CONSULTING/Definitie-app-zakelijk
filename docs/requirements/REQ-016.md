# REQ-016: Nederlandse Juridische Terminologie

## Domain Context

This requirement aligns with Dutch justice sector standards and frameworks:

- **ASTRA Compliance**: Follows ASTRA (Algemene Strategie Referentie Architectuur) guidelines for justice IT systems
- **NORA Principles**: Adheres to Nederlandse Overheid Referentie Architectuur for government-wide interoperability
- **Justice Chain Integration**: Compatible with OM (Openbaar Ministerie), DJI (Dienst JustitiÃ«le Inrichtingen), Justid, and Rechtspraak systems
- **Legal Framework**: Complies with Dutch legal terminology standards and Aanwijzingen voor de regelgeving
- **Data Quality**: Meets justice sector data quality requirements for legal definitions

## Requirement Description

The system must support comprehensive Dutch legal terminology processing, including:
- Recognition and validation of Dutch legal terms
- Proper handling of Latin legal phrases commonly used in Dutch law
- Support for compound Dutch legal terms
- Contextual understanding of legal term variations

## Acceptance Criteria

1. **Term Recognition**: System correctly identifies Dutch legal terms in input
2. **Compound Terms**: Proper parsing of compound Dutch legal terms (e.g., "wederrechtelijk", "onrechtmatig")
3. **Latin Terms**: Support for Latin terms used in Dutch law (e.g., "in dubio pro reo")
4. **Contextual Variations**: Handles different forms and conjugations of legal terms
5. **Domain Specificity**: Distinguishes between general Dutch and legal-specific usage

## Implementation Details

- Term dictionary in `config/toetsregels/`
- Domain model implementation in `src/models/`
- Validation rules for Dutch legal terminology
- Integration with GPT-4 for contextual understanding

## Validation Rules

- ARAI-001: Accurate legal terminology usage
- CON-001: Consistent term usage throughout definition
- ESS-001: Essential legal terms must be present

## Dependencies

- Dutch language models
- Legal terminology database
- GPT-4 API for contextual analysis
